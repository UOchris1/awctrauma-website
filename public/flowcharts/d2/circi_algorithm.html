CIRCI — Adrenal Insufficiency Algorithm

Critical Illness-Related Corticosteroid Insufficiency • ICU Protocol

Phase 1: Risk Assessment
Refractory septic shock despite fluids + vasopressors • Recent/chronic steroid use • Etomidate exposure
Septic shock
refractory to
vasopressors?
Start Empiric Hydrocortisone
Do NOT delay treatment for cosyntropin test
Consider empiric treatment while awaiting results
Phase 2: Treatment Protocol
Hydrocortisone 50mg IV q6h (200mg/day)
or continuous infusion 200mg/24h
± Fludrocortisone 50mcg PO/NG daily (conditional)
Monitor glucose q4h (steroid hyperglycemia)
Consider Cosyntropin Stimulation Test
Draw baseline cortisol → give Cosyntropin 250mcg IV
Draw cortisol at 30 and 60 min
Delta cortisol < 9 mcg/dL = adrenal insufficiency
Random cortisol < 10 mcg/dL = suggestive
⚠ Etomidate causes adrenal suppression for 24–48h.
If patient received etomidate, consider empiric
stress-dose steroids regardless of stim test.
Clinical response
within 24–48h?
Vasopressor wean,
hemodynamic improvement
Phase 3: Taper
Continue hydrocortisone until vasopressors weaned
Then taper over 5–7 days:
50mg q6h → 50mg q8h → 50mg q12h → stop
Do NOT stop abruptly
Reassess Diagnosis
Consider alternative causes of shock
Rule out ongoing septic source
Endocrine consult if unclear
Contraindications: active fungal infection
