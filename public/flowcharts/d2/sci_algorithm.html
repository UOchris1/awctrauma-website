Spinal Cord Injury Algorithm

Acute SCI Management — Level 1 Trauma Center Protocol

Phase 1: 0–20 Minutes
SCI Patient — ED Arrival
Backboard removal • Spinal precautions • ATLS primary survey
Hypotensive?
Hemorrhagic Shock
Tachycardic + Cool extremities
ATLS, FAST, blood products
Neurogenic Shock
Bradycardic + Warm extremities
Norepinephrine preferred
Target MAP 85–90 mmHg
Mixed / Unclear
Treat hemorrhagic FIRST
Volume resuscitate → then vasopressors
Phase 2: 0–2 Hours
ICU Admission
ASIA exam • Arterial line • Foley catheter
MAP goal 85–90 mmHg × 5–7 days
Temperature monitoring (SCI patients are poikilothermic)
Phase 3: 2–6 Hours
MRI if indicated • Spine surgery consult
Assess for cord compression, ligamentous injury
MPSS Decision
Within 8h of injury
(Controversial — shared
decision with patient)
MPSS Elected
30 mg/kg IV bolus
then 5.4 mg/kg/hr × 23h
Monitor for hyperglycemia, infection
MPSS Declined
Document decision + rationale
Continue standard care
Phase 4: 0–24 Hours
Surgical
decompression
indicated?
Decompression
Target: within 24h of injury
Early decompression associated
with improved outcomes
Continue Monitoring
Serial ASIA exams
MAP support ongoing
Phase 5: 24–48 Hours
VTE Prophylaxis
Start chemical prophylaxis at 24–48h
Per weight-based enoxaparin table (see VTE Decision Tree)
Phase 6: 3–7 Days
Ongoing ICU Management
Continue MAP support 85–90
Wean vasopressors as tolerated
Bowel/bladder protocol
Temperature regulation
Respiratory Monitoring
FVC and NIF q4–6h if cervical SCI
Intubate if:
FVC < 15 mL/kg OR NIF weaker than −20
