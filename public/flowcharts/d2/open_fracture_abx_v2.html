Open Fracture — Antibiotic Prophylaxis

Gustilo–Anderson Classification  •  Institutional Guideline TRM_GUI.0031  •  Empiric Regimens

▼
Open Fracture Identified
— ED Arrival

Give antibiotics as early as possible — ideally ≤ 1 h. Do not delay for imaging or transfer. Update tetanus. Classify Gustilo–Anderson type.

Two Questions Drive the Regimen

1. β-lactam allergy?  2. Gustilo type + contamination (soil/fecal or standing water)?  Then add vancomycin if known MRSA colonization.

Add for known MRSA colonizationVancomycin
  • Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h — added to any regimen above for documented MRSA colonization.
  • Pharmacy-dosed; see evidence note on AUC-guided dosing.
Duration
  • Type I / II: 24 hours.
  • Type III: continue until 24 h after final wound closure.
  • No added benefit beyond 24 h post-closure, even for Type III.
▼
Evidence notes — beyond the institutional guideline; current evidence, confirm locally before use
  • Water exposure: piperacillin–tazobactam (per policy) covers Aeromonas (fresh / brackish water). For saltwater or brackish wounds, current ID guidance also favors Doxycycline 100 mg IV/PO q12h + a 3rd-gen cephalosporin (e.g., ceftazidime) for Vibrio vulnificus.
  • Vancomycin: AUC-guided dosing is now preferred over trough targets (ASHP/IDSA 2020) — dose per pharmacy.
  • Timing: earliest antibiotic administration (≤ 1 h) is the factor most strongly associated with reduced infection.
  • β-lactam allergy with contaminated Type III: regimen not specified in policy — consult ID / pharmacy.
▼
Concurrent Care
  • Tetanus prophylaxis per immunization status.
  • Irrigation & debridement — urgent; emergent for gross contamination or vascular compromise.
  • Plan definitive soft-tissue coverage (target ≤ 5–7 days).
  • Re-dose intraoperative antibiotics per blood loss / duration.
