BCVI Screening & Management

Blunt Cerebrovascular Injury • Expanded Denver Criteria • Biffl Grading

Expanded Denver Screening Criteria — Obtain CTA
  • Arterial hemorrhage from neck/nose/mouth
  • Expanding cervical hematoma
  • Cervical bruit in patient < 50
  • Focal neurological deficit (TIA, stroke)
  • Horner syndrome
  • GCS ≤ 8 with no CT explanation
  • Le Fort II or III fracture
  • Basilar skull fracture into carotid canal
  • C-spine fracture (subluxation, fx through foramen transversarium)
  • DAI with GCS < 6
  • Near-hanging with anoxia
  • Seatbelt sign / clothesline injury to neck

Antithrombotic Therapy
Grade I–II: ASA 325mg daily
Grade III–IV: Heparin drip (consider)
or ASA 325mg daily per institutional protocol
Start ASAP when safe — within 24h if possible
Follow-Up Imaging & Monitoring
Repeat CTA at 7 days — assess for progression or resolution
If Grade I resolved → discontinue antithrombotic
If persistent or worsened → continue therapy, repeat CTA at 3 months
Grade III–V: vascular surgery consult for stent/repair consideration
