Difficult Airway Algorithm

Trauma Airway Management • Adapted for Emergency/Trauma Setting

Airway Assessment
LEMON assessment • Predictors: short neck, limited mouth opening, facial trauma, C-spine injury, blood/secretions
⚠ DOUBLE SETUP — Prepare for Surgical Airway Simultaneously
Surgical cricothyrotomy kit open and ready • Call for help early • Designate team roles
RSI — Rapid Sequence Intubation
Pre-oxygenation • Apneic oxygenation (15L NC)
RSI Medications
Ketamine 1.5–2 mg/kg IV
Succinylcholine 1.5 mg/kg IV
or Rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg IV
Avoid etomidate if adrenal concerns
Attempt 1
Video Laryngoscopy
VL as primary device (preferred)
Optimal positioning, external manipulation
Bougie available
Attempt 2
Adjust & Retry
Change blade size/type
Reposition (ear-to-sternal notch)
Bougie-assisted intubation
Different operator
Attempt 3 — MAX
Supraglottic Airway (SGA)
Insert LMA/i-gel as rescue
Oxygenate & ventilate through SGA
Consider intubation through SGA
This is the last attempt before CICO
Successful Intubation
Confirm with ETCO2 waveform
Secure tube • CXR confirmation
Post-intubation sedation/analgesia
Ventilator settings
🚨 DECLARE CICO
Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate
SpO2 falling • Unable to ventilate via SGA
IMMEDIATE SURGICAL AIRWAY
Surgical Cricothyrotomy
Scalpel-bougie-tube technique
Vertical skin incision → horizontal membrane
6.0 cuffed ETT or tracheostomy tube
Confirm with ETCO2 waveform
