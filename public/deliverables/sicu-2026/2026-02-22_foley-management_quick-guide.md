# Trauma/SICU Foley Management Quick Guide

For bedside teams and rounds. Draft for next meeting review.

## Why This Matters

- CAUTI risk rises with longer catheter duration.
- Unnecessary urine cultures and prolonged Foley use drive false diagnoses, antibiotics, and harm.
- Fast removal + clear indications + correct urine culture practice are the highest-yield controls. [R1][R2][R5]

## Use a Foley Only If There Is a Valid Indication

- Acute urinary retention or outlet obstruction.
- Need for accurate hourly urine output in critically ill/unstable patients.
- Perioperative use for selected operations (shortest duration possible).
- Assistance with healing severe sacral/perineal wounds in incontinent patients when alternatives fail.
- Comfort-focused end-of-life care when needed.

If no indication exists, do not place or continue Foley. [R1][R2]

## ICU Arrival Rules (Local Workflow)

- Outside facility/field/ED Foley: remove on ICU arrival unless current indication is documented.
- Trauma Bay sterile Foley may remain when order is explicitly `Provider to Manage`.
- Use `Nurse to Manage` orders for nurse-driven removal when no indication remains.

## Post-Removal Bladder Management (Current Local Draft)

- Bladder scan every 6 hours after removal until spontaneous voiding pattern is established.
- 0-450 mL: observe, rescan in 2 hours.
- 450-700 mL: intermittent straight catheterization (up to 3 attempts).
- >700 mL or repeated failure: notify provider, consider Foley reinsertion.

## Urine Culture Stewardship

- Do not pan-culture from an old/dirty Foley by default.
- Do not collect urine from the drainage bag.
- If CAUTI is suspected and catheter has been in place >14 days and still needed:
- Replace catheter first, then collect specimen from the new catheter sampling port. [R6]
- Avoid treating asymptomatic bacteriuria in catheterized adults (except special indications like pregnancy or urologic mucosal procedures). [R5]

## Daily Rounds Foley Checklist (30 Seconds)

1. Does this patient still meet an approved Foley indication?
2. If yes, is that indication documented today?
3. If no, remove now and start bladder scan pathway.
4. If fever occurs, does urinary source have real signs/symptoms before culturing?

## 30-Day Unit Targets

- >=90% Foley-days with documented indication.
- >=95% removal of outside/ED Foleys on ICU arrival when no indication exists.
- >=30% reduction in non-indicated urine cultures.
- Downward trend in CAUTI event rate and device utilization.
