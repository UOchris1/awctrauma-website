# Adult Trauma/SICU Foley Management Guideline (Evidence-Based Draft)

Version: Draft 1.0  
Date: 2026-02-22  
Scope: Adult Trauma ICU and SICU inpatients

## 1. Purpose

Standardize Foley catheter use, maintenance, and removal to reduce CAUTI, reduce unnecessary cultures/antibiotics, and preserve accurate ICU monitoring when clinically necessary.

## 2. Definitions

- Foley catheter: indwelling urethral urinary catheter.
- CAUTI suspicion: compatible clinical picture where urinary source is plausible.
- Nurse-driven removal order: protocolized authority for bedside removal when indication is absent.

## 3. Core Policy Statements

1. Foley catheters are used only for approved indications and removed at the earliest safe time. [R1][R2]
2. Every Foley requires documented indication at insertion and daily reassessment. [R2]
3. Nurse-driven removal protocols are standard unless a provider explicitly opts for provider-managed catheterization. [R2][R7]
4. Urine cultures are ordered only when clinically indicated and are collected using correct sterile technique. [R2][R3][R6]
5. Asymptomatic bacteriuria is not treated in most catheterized adults. [R5]

## 4. Approved Indications for Indwelling Foley

- Acute urinary retention or bladder outlet obstruction.
- Need for accurate urine output in critically ill or hemodynamically unstable patients.
- Selected perioperative indications (short duration, remove early).
- Assistance in healing severe sacral/perineal wounds in incontinent patients when alternatives are inadequate.
- End-of-life comfort if needed.

If indication is absent, Foley should not be present. [R1][R2]

## 5. Insertion and Initial Orders

1. Perform hand hygiene and aseptic insertion by trained personnel. [R1][R2]
2. Record date/time of insertion, inserter, and explicit indication. [R2]
3. Select one order mode:
- `Provider to Manage`: provider retains direct control.
- `Nurse to Manage`: nurse-driven protocolized removal when indication resolves.

## 6. ICU Admission Handling for Existing Foleys

1. Outside facility, prehospital, or ED Foleys:
- Remove on ICU arrival unless a current approved indication is documented.
2. Trauma Bay sterile Foley:
- May remain if indication is present and order is explicitly `Provider to Manage`.

## 7. Maintenance Bundle

1. Maintain closed drainage system; keep unobstructed urine flow. [R1][R2]
2. Keep sampling and tubing technique sterile.
3. Do not break the system unless clinically required.
4. Do not perform routine scheduled catheter changes without indication. [R1]
5. Reassess need for catheter daily during rounds and nursing review. [R2]

## 8. Removal and Post-Removal Bladder Protocol

### 8.1 Trigger for removal

- Immediate removal when no approved indication remains.

### 8.2 Local post-removal pathway (current ICU draft from meeting minutes)

- Bladder scan q6h.
- 0-450 mL: observe and rescan in 2h.
- 450-700 mL: intermittent catheterization (up to 3 episodes).
- >700 mL or repeated failure: provider notification and consider reinsertion.

### 8.3 Evidence alignment note

External toolkits often use lower post-void residual thresholds for intermittent catheterization (for example around >300 mL in sample protocols). [R8]  
Recommendation: retain current thresholds short-term but track retention and reinsertion outcomes for 60 days, then recalibrate.

## 9. Urine Culture and Antibiotic Stewardship

1. Do not order reflex pan-cultures after a single isolated fever without clinical context. [R2][R3]
2. Do not collect urine from drainage bag. [R3]
3. If symptomatic CAUTI is suspected and catheter has been in place >14 days and still indicated:
- Replace catheter first.
- Collect urine culture from freshly placed catheter sampling port before antibiotics. [R6]
4. Avoid treatment of asymptomatic bacteriuria in catheterized adults unless guideline exceptions apply. [R5]

## 10. Metrics and Audit Plan

Track monthly:

1. Foley device utilization ratio.
2. Percent Foley-days with documented indication.
3. Percent outside/ED Foleys removed on ICU arrival.
4. Urine culture orders per 100 catheter-days.
5. CAUTI events and SIR.

## 11. Implementation Plan (First 30 Days)

1. Build/update order panel with explicit `Provider to Manage` and `Nurse to Manage` options.
2. Publish quick-reference handout and post in ICU workrooms.
3. Educate resident, nursing, APP, and RT teams (15-minute micro-sessions).
4. Start weekly audit with direct feedback on non-indicated Foley continuation and culture orders.

## 12. Approval Needed at Next Meeting

3. Named owners and go-live date.
