# Foley/CAUTI Evidence Summary

Prepared: 2026-02-22  
Purpose: Source list supporting the draft pamphlet and Foley management guideline.

## Core References

### [R1] CDC HICPAC CAUTI Recommendations (updated 2024)

- Source: CDC, Summary of Recommendations for prevention of CAUTI.
- Why used: Foundational U.S. prevention framework for indications, insertion, maintenance, and early removal.
- Key points used:
- Use catheters only for appropriate indications.
- Minimize duration.
- Maintain aseptic insertion and closed drainage.
- Do not change catheters/bags at fixed intervals unless clinically indicated.
- Link: https://www.cdc.gov/infection-control/hcp/cauti/summary-of-recommendations.html

### [R2] SHEA/IDSA/APIC Compendium, CAUTI Chapter (2022 update; published 2023)

- Source: Strategies to prevent CAUTI in acute-care hospitals.
- Why used: Contemporary specialty society essential practices for hospital programs.
- Key points used:
- Document indication and insertion/removal data.
- Use nurse-driven removal protocols.
- Daily reassessment and timely removal.
- Improve urine culture stewardship and avoid unnecessary cultures.
- Link: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/infection-control-and-hospital-epidemiology/article/strategies-to-prevent-catheterassociated-urinary-tract-infections-in-acutecare-hospitals-2022-update/EF0D75DEB2A1BEA8E17BFCA8A95AE2F6

### [R3] CDC Urine Culture Stewardship Clinical Guidance

- Source: CDC UTI stewardship pages for clinicians.
- Why used: Practical recommendations to reduce unnecessary urine culturing.
- Key points used:
- Do not culture without clinical indication.
- Avoid collecting from drainage bags.
- Improve collection and ordering behavior with unit workflows.
- Links:
- https://www.cdc.gov/uti/hcp/clinical-guidance/index.html
- https://www.cdc.gov/uti/hcp/uti-stewardship/strategize-initiatives.html

### [R4] CDC UTI Stewardship Implementation Actions

- Source: CDC STRATEGIZE initiative summary.
- Why used: Unit-level intervention ideas for urine culture stewardship.
- Key points used:
- Implement protocols and education to reduce non-indicated urine cultures.
- Link: https://www.cdc.gov/uti/hcp/uti-stewardship/strategize-initiatives.html

### [R5] IDSA Guideline: Asymptomatic Bacteriuria (2019)

- Source: IDSA clinical practice guideline update.
- Why used: Defines when not to screen/treat bacteriuria in catheterized adults.
- Key points used:
- Recommends against screening/treating asymptomatic bacteriuria in patients with long-term indwelling catheters.
- Supports limiting unnecessary antibiotics and cultures.
- Link: https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/asymptomatic-bacteriuria/

### [R6] IDSA Guideline: Catheter-Associated UTI (2009)

- Source: International clinical practice guideline for CAUTI.
- Why used: Still-cited procedural guidance for specimen collection in chronic catheters.
- Key points used:
- If catheter has been in place more than about 2 weeks and remains indicated, replace catheter before obtaining culture and before treatment decisions.
- Link: https://academic.oup.com/cid/article/50/5/625/324341

### [R7] Trauma ICU Evidence: Nurse-Driven Foley Removal

- Source: Tyson AF et al., Journal of Trauma and Acute Care Surgery (2020).
- Why used: Trauma ICU-specific outcome data.
- Key points used:
- Nurse-driven Foley removal protocol was associated with lower catheter utilization and lower CAUTI incidence.
- Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31923080/

### [R8] AHRQ Toolkit Sample Bladder Management Protocol Language

- Source: AHRQ CAUTI toolkit sample policy/protocol documents.
- Why used: Operational examples for bladder scan and intermittent catheter pathways.
- Key points used:
- Provides practical pathway examples for post-removal urinary retention management.
- Link: https://www.ahrq.gov/hai/cauti-tools/guides/implguide-pt3b.html

### [R9] Quality Study: Urine Culture Stewardship + CAUTI Reduction

- Source: Frontera JA et al., American Journal of Infection Control (2021).
- Why used: Demonstrates impact of reducing unnecessary urine cultures in ICU settings.
- Key points used:
- Study reported substantial reduction in CAUTI events and associated costs after restricting inappropriate urine cultures.
- Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33775970/

## Notes

1. [R1]-[R4] were prioritized for core policy language.
2. [R5]-[R9] were used for implementation details and ICU workflow support.
3. Post-removal bladder scan thresholds should be treated as locally validated policy, then tuned with 30-60 day outcome data.
